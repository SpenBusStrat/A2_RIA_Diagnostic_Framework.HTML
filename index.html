How to Use This Framework

A guide for A2 Investment Partners colleagues using this tool in client conversations.

Purpose

This framework gives A2 Investment Partners a repeatable, structured way to assess RIA firm health in every client conversation. It serves two goals: (1) identify where the firm has critical gaps that specialists or solutions can address, and (2) create a credible, data-driven basis for product and service recommendations.

The output of every conversation should be a completed Discovery Framework with notes and ratings — which auto-generates dimension scores and an overall health score that flows directly into the Scorecard tab.

Recommended Workflow

1
Pre-meeting prep: Review the Top 10 Challenges for the firm's segment. Note which are most likely given what you already know.
2
During the conversation: Open the Discovery Framework tab. Enter the firm name and date. Work through each dimension — type notes as the client speaks and rate each answer 1–10. You don't need to be linear; jump to the dimensions most relevant to the conversation.
3
Rating each question: Rate the firm's current state for that dimension — not their answer quality. 1–3 = significant gap or risk, 4–6 = partial or developing capability, 7–8 = solid and functional, 9–10 = best-in-class or fully optimized.
4
Review the summary: The Assessment Summary at the bottom of the Framework tab compiles dimension scores live. The Scorecard tab syncs these scores automatically. Use both to identify the top 2–3 priority areas.
5
Export: Click "Export Notes" to copy a full text summary of all answers, ratings, and scores to your clipboard for pasting into CRM notes, email, or a follow-up document.

Scoring Guidance

8–10: Strong. Acknowledge as a strength and move on.

5–7: Needs attention. Medium-term roadmap items once critical issues are addressed.

1–4: Priority risk. Lead with these. A score below 4 in compliance, operations, or succession represents material risk.

Dimension scores are the average of the five question ratings within that dimension. The overall score is the average across all seven dimensions.
